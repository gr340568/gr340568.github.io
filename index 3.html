Why lobster became so popular

what made it so popular? Its rich flavour? Or its amount of meat? The answer is that... it all starts at the peer influence. Lobster didn't become famous because its taste, but because of the influence that people gave after eating it. The railroad company had served this crustacean to inland people because they have not witnessed this exotic meal, those who were not aware of this ate it, and enjoyed the flavour. The chef realized that it look more appetizing and tasted better, and started to serve lobsters in 1880s, as a side dish just like bread and butter pickles in in the 1850s and '60s. As the population of lobster decreased, the price had risen up, making it high priced. But still in great depression, poor families in maine sneaked at night and caught lobsters, showing how it still had its reputation as a food for the poor. but during ww2, people started to eat more lobster, and their value increased. By 1950s, they were seen as foods that rich people had served to their friends, or what movie stars ate during dinner, and this reputation still lasts till this day Not all people love them however, as they were still seen as the cockroaches of the sea.
